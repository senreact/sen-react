13. Step by step — what are the real steps to formalise a business in Senegal today? (Walk it as if we were a young entrepreneur starting from scratch.)
    Here are the key steps for an entrepreneur starting from scratch

        •	Formalising one's business in Senegal is done mainly through the Business Creation Bureau (BCE) of APIX in Dakar or the regional bureau if you live in the regions. The Dakar bureau centralises the procedures in 48 hours. The process includes registration with the RCCM, obtaining the NINEA, and the declaration of existence. One must distinguish the individual enterprise (simple) from the company (SARL/SA), the latter requiring a notary

    Steps and documents for formalisation:
    Individual Enterprise (Natural person):

- 2 copies of the national identity card or passport (for foreigners).
- Criminal record dated less than 3 months ago or sworn declaration.
- Certificate of residence.
- Form to fill out at APIX.
- Payment of Creation Costs

Company (SARL, SUARL, SA):

- Drafting of the articles of association (often by a notary).
- Declaration of subscription and payment (share capital).
- Minutes of incorporation.
- Signature of the partners.
  After filing
  Receipt of an acknowledgement.
- Registration (RCCM/NINEA): Registration of the business.
- Declaration of existence: Tax registration.
- Declaration of opening of establishment: Social formality.

      •	To obtain the Authorisation of Manufacturing and Sale (FRA) in Senegal, indispensable for food products, one must submit a file to the Division of Consumer and Consumer Safety (DCSC), at Camp Lat Dior, Dakar. The process involves the submission of samples, labelling, and the compliance of premises.

  Documents required (FRA File)

- Application addressed to the head of division of the DCSC (specifying the product, the address, the manufacturing process).
- 4 samples of the product for analysis.
- Mock-up of label/packaging.
- Certificate of analysis of the product.
- Copy of the RCCM (Trade and Personal Property Credit Register).
- Articles of association of the business (legal entity).

Internal Trade Directorate
Steps of the procedure

- Filing: The complete file is to be filed with the DCSC or with the regional Trade service.
- Analyses: Microbiological/chemical analyses are carried out (costs at the applicant's expense).
- Inspection: An inspection of the premises may be carried out to check hygiene standards.
- Validation: Once the product is judged compliant and safe, the FRA number is assigned.
- The cost varies according to the analyses, but the file requires a tax stamp, file fees, and the cost of analyses.
- The authorisation is valid as long as the production activity is carried on.
  NB: It is mandatory to report any modification of formula or labelling.

• To obtain an import-export card in Senegal, one must be registered in the Trade Register (RCCM), have a NINEA and a trader's card. The application is filed with the Internal Trade Directorate (DCI) or with the regional Chambers of Commerce with a file including the trader's card, a photo and tax stamps.
Key Steps

- Registration: Create your business and obtain your NINEA (from APIX) and the Trade Register.
- Trader's Card: Obtain the trader's card from the Chamber of Commerce, Industry and Agriculture of Dakar (CCIAD) or in the region.
- Filing of the file: Constitute the file and file it with the Internal Trade Directorate.
  Necessary Documents
- A certified true copy of the trader's card.
- A copy of the NINEA.
- A certified true copy of the Trade Register.
- An identity photo.
- A tax stamp (often 10,000 FCFA).
- A receipt of payment to the Senegalese Shippers' Council (COSEC)
